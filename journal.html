Heavy Traces

I open a midnight window
to wet summer air and
sweet cicada song.
And there's that familiar thrum
of parents who pretend to love.
And there's the boy
with the quiet way about him,
and Marigold who is my friend
only when she calls,
and there’s my aunt
who hangs horseshoes and
emblems of hate,
and the cicadas
who only emerge from the dirt to sing and love

then die.

So don’t bother asking me
what is the point of this life.

I remember my Granny,
who knocks down
hornet nests,
while I watch with my brother
on quilted spreads,
tasting her cigarette perfume
through an open window.

Krish

Once, I loved winter
But I’ve grown impatient for summer
For sun on our skin
For you to be here,
The space between us
No more than a medley
Of soft words and
Warm tones
And lips upon lips.
I think if you were here
We’d sip wine from melted sand.
You’d tie my bathing suit,
And brush my hair
And I’d tell you how things are,
Honestly
I think if you were here
I’d never be impatient again

Peach Tea

It was raining while we swam in the ocean, your hands on my waist. The sun was setting
behind a wall of gray. Everything was gray, except for your eyes. The warmest, brownest eyes
I’d ever seen sitting perfectly on either side of your nose. Your black curls
dripped with saltwater, hung over your forehead like fronds after a storm.
Brown and gray and black and gray and brown. And you were shivering.

Could you tell I wanted to kiss you? Did I tell you just how badly I wanted to kiss you? I can’t remember.
I do remember that it was our last day together. That I rested my head on your shoulder and closed my eyes, knowing
you had to go, wishing we could stay this way forever.
